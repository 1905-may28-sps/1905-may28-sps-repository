Grenada

Spice of the Caribbean

Grenada is a Caribbean country comprising a main island, and two smaller islands; Carriacou and Petite Martinique!.

Dubbed the “Spice Isle,” Grenada a place where untouched beauty meets our warm peoplecontent with a simple way of life. Grenada offers a lifestyle so pure and authentic that you feel instantly renewed.

Our enchanting islands boasts silky stretches of white sand beaches, sunken treasures adorned by corals and unspoilt landscapes undiscovered by crowds. So, take a journey like never before; a journey that is pure and real.

Everything about this majestic island is a reflection of it's people, even the colors of their national flag. The red colour of the flag stands for courage and vitality of our people, gold for their wisdom and warmth, and green for the lush vegetation and agriculture.


CULTURE


Grenadian culture is a mixture of British, African, West Indian and French influences which has left an indomitable influence in the folklore, dialect, music and general way of life. French influence on Grenadian culture can still be found in surnames, names of villages and the local dialect or Patois.
Grenadian culture is one that is deaply rooted in Religion and Music. Almost every religion or musical trend is a mixture of two or more cultures coming together to create something truly special. About 65 percent of Grenadians are Roman Catholic. Most of the rest belong to Protestant denominations which include Anglican, Methodist, Seventh-Day Adventist, and Baptist. Most of Grenada's small Indian population is Hindu. Shango, a traditional African religion, is still practiced, generally in combination with Christian beliefs. African religious practices are especially prominent on the small island of Carriacou.

Popular Attractions


  • Grand Anse Beach
    • Grand Anse Beach offers a magical formula: perfect white sand, contrasted by the inviting turquoise Caribbean Sea, fringed by coconut and sea-almond trees, all with the backdrop of Grenada’s verdant mountains and the picturesque St George’s Harbour.
    • The juxtaposition of colours, the sound of the gentle waves, the feel of the sand between your toes, and the salty smell of the ocean, is a treat for all your senses.

  • Belmont Estate
    • Belmont Estate is the premiere tourism attraction on the Spice Isle – Grenada. Located in St Patrick only an hour’s scenic drive from the island’s capital, St. George, Belmont Estate is a unique and authentic 17th century plantation that offers guests an opportunity to participate in and observe the workings of a fully functional historic plantation.
    • guests can enjoy exciting tours that include visits to our organic farm, gardens, heritage museum, and cocoa processing facilities. We also offer an exquisite restaurant featuring traditional Grenadian cuisine, a goat dairy farm, petting farm, conference room, gift shop, café, produce shop, credit union and craft market.
    • This is a place where you can truly enjoy the best of true Grenadian hospitality, traditional rural life, the beauty, tranquillity and diversity of our breathtaking surroundings.
